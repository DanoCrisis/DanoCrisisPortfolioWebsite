Favourite Games

Lets take a look at some gaming gems from over the years, that are my particular favourites.


Of course we must start with CAPCOM's Dino Crisis.


Released in 1999 for the PlayStation 1; Dino Crisis was developed by the same team that had worked on the successful Resident Evil game 3 years earlier.

This time though they decided to create a game that was a little more "panic horror" than "survival horror".

Thus they introduced a quick, intellegent enemy; the raptors!

These little beauties could; track your blood trail, open doors, and work together to trap you.

And once they had you cornered, they could bite your arms, tail whip you to the ground, or even leap upon you; pinning you to the floor.


But the game was more than just these terrifying creatures:

  • It had a fantastic, more realistic inventory system, that allowed you to combine items to make powerful healing
    items or even poison darts.
  • The plot was well written and the voice acting was top notch.
  • It even had multiple endings based on the choices you made throughout the game.
  • Possibly best aspect however was the amazing puzzles; that had you rerouting power, shifting crates away with
    cranes, and hacking into computer systems to open escape tunnels.

When you put all of this together, you had a fast-paced, nerve wracking, horror game that kept you coming back
for more.
Oh and did I mention it had a T-rex?

It had a T-rex!


Next we are going a little retro, back to the Commodore 64, with Boulderdash.


Released in 1984 for Atari 8-bit computers (although later released on the Commodore 64); Boulderdash was created by Canadian developers Peter Liepa and Chris Gray.

A straightforward 2D puzzle game; you simply had to navigate you character "Rockford" around the cave and collect diamonds in order to open the exit door.
Of course it was never quite as easy as that.

Each cave was timed, meaning you had to rush all of your actions, in order to make it out alive.
There were two enemies: "Fireflies" and "Butterflies" that would chase you relentlessly.

And as if that wasn't enough, not only could rocks land on your head and kill you, but they could also completly surround and trap you in certain circumstance.


What I'm describing probably sounds quite basic, (and it was), however it had some stand out features:

  • Despite the limitations of the time its enemies actually had basic A.I. the "Fireflies would chase you up and
    left, whereas "Butterflies" would chase you down and right, this made them feel more real and terrifying!
  • Even though there were only about 12 basic components, the permitations for level design were almost endless, giving
    it a great variety between puzzle and action.
  • Its best feature for me was released along with Boulderdash 3, this contained the "Construction Kit" which allowed
    you to design, save and play your own levels.

That last feature was a total game changer for me, and was probably what got me thinking about game development from a
young age.

Overall it was a simple game that contained many fun features to keep you playing again and again.
If only you could save your progress back then, it would have been perfect!
